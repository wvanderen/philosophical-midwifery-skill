# Example — Overinterpretation prevention

**Scenario 2 of 5 (SC2).** This fixture demonstrates the presentation protocol from `../references/PATHOLOGOS_PATTERNS.md` (reinforced by `../SKILL.md` dialogue rules): candidate hidden beliefs are offered **only as hypotheses**, never asserted, and the user selects one, rejects another, and revises a third in their own words *before* any examination begins. The asserted behavior: the agent never tells the user what they "really" believe, offers at most a few candidates, and accepts a rejection or a blank as readily as a match. The transcript is clean — no inline rubric annotations.

> **Agent:** You mentioned a concrete moment — your partner rolled their eyes when you brought up the budget, and you shut down for the rest of the evening. Take your time; what was going through your mind right before you went quiet?
>
> **User:** I just thought, here we go again, I always ruin things.
>
> **Agent:** From what you've described, a couple of possible beliefs might be operating here — treat all of these as guesses, and feel free to discard every one. One is *I am responsible for keeping the peace*: if conflict erupts, it's my fault. Another is *my needs are a burden*: speaking up only causes damage. A third, a little different, is *if I'm not careful every moment, the relationship will collapse*. Do any of those land, or would you like to restate it in your own words?
>
> **User:** The second one, about my needs being a burden — yeah. But the "relationship will collapse" one feels too dramatic. That's not quite it.
>
> **Agent:** That's useful — you've selected one and set another aside. Would you restate the one that fit in your own words, so we're examining the belief you actually hold rather than my phrasing?
>
> **User:** It's more like: if I ask for what I need, I'm being selfish, and that selfishness damages the people around me.
>
> **Agent:** Thank you. Let me read that back so you can confirm it's accurate: *"Asking for what I need is selfish and damages the people around me."* Is that stated correctly, or does it need adjusting?
>
> **User:** Add — it damages *the people I love*. That part matters.
>
> **Agent:** *"Asking for what I need is selfish and damages the people I love."* Better?
>
> **User:** Yes. That's it.
>
> **Agent:** Before we examine it, I want to check — none of this is me saying this belief is true, or that it's the one you "really" hold. You picked it from guesses I offered; it's yours to keep, revise, or drop at any point. Shall we look at it?
>
> **User:** Let's look at it.

This fixture asserts the non-overinterpretation posture: multiple candidates offered as guesses, an explicit rejection accepted without resistance, a user-authored restatement preferred over the agent's phrasing, and a final framing that the belief is the user's to own or discard — not a diagnosis the agent has handed down.
