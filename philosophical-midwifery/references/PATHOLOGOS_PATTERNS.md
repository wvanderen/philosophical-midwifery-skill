# Pathologos Patterns

This is your reference for generating candidate hidden beliefs during philosophical midwifery. Read it when the user has given a concrete example of a disturbance and you need candidate beliefs to offer. Obey the presentation protocol below before you use the archetype catalog.

## What "pathologos" means here

A **pathologos** is the belief beneath the suffering — the generalized proposition operating under a felt disturbance. It is the hidden claim that gives the disturbance its charge.

A pathologos is a **hypothesis, never a diagnosis.** You are inferring a possible belief, not identifying a condition. The user is always the authority on whether a candidate fits.

## Presentation protocol (binding — read before offering any candidate)

These rules govern every candidate belief you offer. They reinforce the dialogue rules in `../SKILL.md`.

1. **Offer candidates ONLY as hypotheses.** Phrase them as guesses: "One possible belief that might be operating here is… treat this as a hypothesis, not a conclusion." Never assert what the user believes.
2. **Never assert what the user believes.** No "You believe X." The user's interior is not yours to state.
3. **The user ALWAYS selects, rejects, or revises before any examination begins.** Ask which candidate fits (if any), or invite them to restate the belief in their own words. No examination starts until the user owns the proposition being tested.
4. **If none fit, say so plainly** and ask the user to restate the belief themselves. A blank is better than a forced fit.
5. **Prefer at most two or three candidates at a time.** A long list overwhelms and over-interprets.

## The four v1 archetypes

Use these as generative lenses, not a checklist. A disturbance may fit more than one, or none. The archetypes are grounded in two established frameworks — Burns's cognitive distortions and Ellis's REBT irrational beliefs — which supply the propositions, distortions, and leverage points below.

### Conditional worth

- **Core generalized proposition:** "My worth as a person depends on some condition — achievement, recognition, or others' approval."
- **Concrete phrasings a user might say:**
  - "If my work goes unrecognized, it has no value."
  - "If I fail at this, I'm nothing."
  - "Being overlooked means I don't matter."
- **Typical affective charge:** shame; resentment; a hollow feeling even on success; dread of invisibility.
- **Distortion / leverage point to examine:** global human-worth rating (REBT *depreciation*) fused with all-or-nothing thinking, disqualifying the positive, and "should" statements (Burns). The leverage is to separate *worth* from the *condition* — is recognition required for worth, or merely a signal of it?

### Control & responsibility

- **Core generalized proposition:** "I must control, or am responsible for, something I cannot in fact control."
- **Concrete phrasings a user might say:**
  - "It's my fault they reacted that way."
  - "If I just try harder, I can make this turn out right."
  - "I should have been able to prevent it."
- **Typical affective charge:** guilt; anxiety; rage; burnout; helplessness.
- **Distortion / leverage point to examine:** personalization and blaming, catastrophizing, the fallacy of change, and control fallacies (Burns) fused with REBT *demands* ("must/should"), *awfulizing*, and *low frustration tolerance*. The leverage is domain separation — what is and is not actually within the user's control — and testing necessity/sufficiency.

### Belonging & rejection

- **Core generalized proposition:** "Rejection or exclusion means I am unworthy or unlovable."
- **Concrete phrasings a user might say:**
  - "If they don't want me, there's something wrong with me."
  - "Being left out proves I'm not enough."
  - "They must approve of me for me to be okay."
- **Typical affective charge:** hurt; jealousy; social anxiety; shame; withdrawal.
- **Distortion / leverage point to examine:** mind reading, fortune-telling, overgeneralizing, and emotional reasoning (Burns) fused with REBT *approval-musts* ("others MUST treat me well / accept me"). The leverage is the counterexample (a rejection that did not diminish worth) and separating social *status* from *worth*.

### Perfection & flawlessness

- **Core generalized proposition:** "I must be flawless; mistakes are catastrophic."
- **Concrete phrasings a user might say:**
  - "If it isn't perfect, it's worthless."
  - "Making a mistake means I'm incompetent."
  - "I can't start until I know I'll get it right."
- **Typical affective charge:** anxiety; harsh self-criticism; procrastination; shame on any error.
- **Distortion / leverage point to examine:** all-or-nothing thinking, "should" statements, mental filtering, and catastrophizing (Burns) fused with REBT *performance-musts* ("I MUST perform outstandingly and win approval"). The leverage is necessity/sufficiency (is flawlessness required, or merely valuable?), counterexamples, and reformulation of the standard.

## Worked example — "recognition determines worth"

User: "I feel resentful when people don't recognize my work."

Following the presentation protocol, you offer candidates as hypotheses — never assertions:

> **Agent:** "One possible belief that might be operating here — treat this as a guess — is **recognition determines worth**: that the value of your work depends on someone noticing it. Another candidate is **being overlooked means being diminished**. Do either of those fit? You can pick one, revise it, or say neither is right and restate it yourself."
>
> **User:** "The first one, mostly. But it's more that if no one notices, it's like it didn't happen."

The user has now selected and revised. You then normalize the chosen belief into a general proposition (e.g., "The value of my work depends on recognition") and move to `QUESTION_TAXONOMY.md` for examination. If at any point a crisis signal appears, switch to `SAFETY_BOUNDARIES.md` instead.
